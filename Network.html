üõ†Ô∏è Our 8-Phase Network Testing Methodology

Establishing clear testing boundaries and authorization.

  • Define target IP ranges and network segments
  • Determine testing type: External, Internal, or Wireless
  • Establish timelines and access requirements
  • Complete authorization forms and legal agreements
  • Set communication protocols for incident reporting

Comprehensive reconnaissance to map the network environment.

  • Discover live hosts and reachable devices
  • Identify open ports and exposed services
  • Perform banner grabbing and service fingerprinting
  • Collect external DNS, WHOIS, and subdomain records
  • Tools: Nmap, Netdiscover, Masscan, Shodan, Recon-ng

Identifying security weaknesses across network components.

  • Scan operating systems (Windows, Linux, Unix)
  • Check network services (SMB, RDP, FTP, Telnet, SSH, HTTP)
  • Audit firewalls, routers, and VPN configurations
  • Test DNS, DHCP, and authentication services
  • Tools: Nessus, OpenVAS, Nmap scripts, SMBenum, SNMPwalk

Safely simulating attacks against identified vulnerabilities.

  • SMB and RDP brute force attempts
  • Password reuse and credential spraying
  • Man-in-the-middle (MITM) attack simulations
  • Exploiting known CVEs (e.g., EternalBlue, PrintNightmare)
  • Command execution and privilege escalation tests
  • All exploits coordinated with your security team

Demonstrating the potential impact of compromised access.

  • Credential and hash dumping (Mimikatz, LSASS)
  • Password reuse for lateral movement
  • Active Directory trust relationship enumeration
  • Privilege escalation attempts
  • Pivoting from workstations to critical servers
  • Documenting attack paths and potential damage

Validating the effectiveness of network segmentation controls.

  • Test segmentation between zones (guest, production, admin)
  • Identify firewall ACL and rule vulnerabilities
  • Check DMZ boundary effectiveness
  • Test for VLAN hopping and port forwarding issues
  • Verify NAT and routing configurations

Comprehensive documentation of findings and recommendations.

  • Executive Summary for leadership
  • Technical Details of all vulnerabilities
  • Screenshots and Proof-of-Concepts (PoCs)
  • CWE/CVE references with CVSS risk ratings
  • Clear, actionable remediation guidelines
  • Prioritized recommendations based on risk

Ensuring vulnerabilities are properly remediated.

  • Free retest of all identified vulnerabilities
  • Verification of implemented fixes
  • Identification of any still-vulnerable issues
  • Check for newly introduced vulnerabilities
  • Updated final report with closure status
