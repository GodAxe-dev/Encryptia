A Vulnerability Assessment (VA) is a critical step in maintaining your organization's security posture. It involves systematically scanning your systems, networks, and applications to identify known vulnerabilities before attackers do.

Unlike penetration testing, which simulates real attacks, VA focuses on detection and risk analysis of security weaknesses across your infrastructure‚Äîwithout active exploitation.

üõ†Ô∏è Our Vulnerability Assessment Methodology

Comprehensive identification of all assets within the agreed scope.

  • Servers, Desktops, Routers, Firewalls, Switches
  • Web Applications, APIs, Databases, and Cloud Services
  • Internal/External IP ranges
  • Public-facing and internal systems
  • Tools: Nmap, Masscan, Network Inventory Tools

Thorough scanning using industry-leading tools to identify security weaknesses.

  • Unpatched software or OS vulnerabilities
  • Insecure services and protocols (e.g., FTP, SMBv1, Telnet)
  • Default configurations or credentials
  • Missing security headers or SSL issues
  • CMS/Plugin vulnerabilities (e.g., WordPress, Joomla)
  • Outdated or vulnerable libraries
  • Tools: Nessus, OpenVAS, Qualys, Nikto, Nexpose, custom scripts

Prioritizing vulnerabilities based on their potential impact and exploitability.

  • CVSS (Common Vulnerability Scoring System) scoring
  • Risk level classification:
    Critical High Medium Low Informational
  • CVE (Common Vulnerabilities and Exposures) tracking
  • CWE (Common Weakness Enumeration) references
  • Business context consideration

Reducing false positives through expert verification of findings.

  • Manual verification of critical and high-risk vulnerabilities
  • Contextual understanding of risks
  • Elimination of scanner false positives
  • Validation of exploitability
  • Business impact assessment

Clear, actionable reporting for both technical and executive audiences.

  • Executive Summary (for non-technical management)
  • Technical Findings with affected assets
  • CVSS-based risk scoring
  • CWE/CVE references
  • Screenshots or scan evidence (where applicable)
  • Fix recommendations for each issue
  • Prioritized remediation roadmap

Supporting your team in addressing identified vulnerabilities.

  • Understanding root causes
  • Applying vendor or system patches
  • Validating configuration fixes
  • Optional follow-up scan to confirm resolution
  • Remediation verification reporting
